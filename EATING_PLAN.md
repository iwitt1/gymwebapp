# Eating Plan — Flexible Fat-Loss Framework

**Goal:** Lose fat slowly while keeping muscle. Built for mix-and-match so you never have to rebuild it — swap items week to week.

**Avoids:** Dairy (butter OK; **no yogurt, no cheese as a main ingredient**), seafood, peanuts.
**Sourced from:** Trader Joe's + Safeway, leaning on pre-made / pre-marinated items.

---

## Your Targets

| | Per day |
|---|---|
| **Calories** | ~2,100–2,200 |
| **Protein** | ~170g (this is the muscle-protection number — hit it daily) |
| **Carbs** | ~200–230g |
| **Fat** | ~60–70g |

**Why these numbers:** At ~165 lb with regular training, maintenance is roughly 2,400–2,500 cal. We're pulling ~250–300 below that for ~0.5 lb/week of loss — slow enough that your body burns fat instead of muscle, and gentle enough that your lifts don't tank. Protein at ~1g per lb of bodyweight is the single biggest lever for holding muscle in a deficit.

**The only two rules you have to remember:**
1. Hit protein (~170g). Everything else is flexible.
2. Fill half your plate with vegetables. They're near-free on calories and keep you full.

---

## How To Use This (the mix-and-match part)

Every meal is built from the same formula:

> **Protein anchor + Carb + Vegetables + Sauce/Fat**

Pick one from each column and you've got a meal. Rotate columns week to week and nothing gets boring. Each meal below already lands near its calorie/protein target, so you don't have to count — just build.

Rough per-meal targets if you're eating 3 meals + 1 snack:

| Meal | Calories | Protein |
|---|---|---|
| Breakfast | ~450–500 | ~35g |
| Lunch | ~550–600 | ~50g |
| Dinner | ~650–700 | ~55g |
| Snack (optional) | ~150–250 | ~15–20g |

## Breakfast Menu (~450–500 cal, ~35g protein)

Pick any. #1–3 are 5-minute weekday options; #4–5 prep ahead and freeze/store.

1. **Savory scramble** — 3 whole eggs + ¾ cup (6 oz) egg whites, cooked in ½ tbsp butter, 1–2 handfuls spinach, + 2 chicken sausage links *or* ½ tube soy chorizo. Side: 1 piece fruit or 1 slice sourdough.
2. **Egg + potato hash** — 3 eggs + ½ tube soy chorizo + 1 cup frozen diced potatoes, all one pan.
3. **Protein smoothie** — 1 scoop (~1.3 oz) **MRM egg white protein** + 1 banana + ½ cup frozen berries + 1 cup soy/oat/almond milk + 1 tbsp **almond or sunflower butter** (peanut-free). Fast, portable.
4. **High-protein overnight oats** (dairy-free) — ½ cup dry oats + 1 scoop MRM egg white protein + ¾ cup soy milk + 1 tbsp chia + ½ cup berries. Make 3–4 jars at once.
5. **Freezer breakfast burritos** — per burrito: 2 eggs + ¼ tube soy chorizo + ⅓ cup potato + ¼ cup peppers in a tortilla. Batch 6, freeze, microwave 2 min.
6. **Tofu scramble** (cheapest) — ½ block (7 oz) crumbled tofu + ¼ tsp turmeric + 1 cup veg, in a tortilla or over ¾ cup potatoes.

## Lunch Menu (~550–600 cal, ~50g protein)

Assembly bowls and wraps — all keep 3–4 days in the fridge, eat cold or microwaved.

1. **Burrito bowl** — 6 oz taco-seasoned ground turkey + ¾ cup cooked rice + ½ cup peppers/corn + 3 tbsp salsa + 2 tbsp guac.
2. **Teriyaki chicken bowl** — 6 oz Just Chicken + 2 tbsp TJ's **Soyaki** + ¾ cup rice + 1½ cups frozen stir-fry veg.
3. **Mediterranean bowl** — 6 oz chicken + ¾ cup cooked quinoa + 1 cup roasted veg + 2 tbsp hummus + ~10 olives (skip the feta).
4. **Chimichurri steak bowl** — 5 oz carne asada + ¾ cup rice or 1 roasted potato + 1–2 cups arugula + 1½ tbsp chimichurri.
5. **Coconut curry bowl** — 6 oz chicken + ⅓ cup **dairy-free** coconut curry simmer sauce (check label) + ¾ cup rice.
6. **Turkey wrap combo** — 4 oz deli turkey + 2 tbsp hummus + veg in 1 tortilla, + 1 piece fruit + 2 hard-boiled eggs on the side.

**Carbs to rotate:** frozen brown/jasmine rice, quinoa, sweet potato, sourdough, tortillas, pasta, farro.
**Veg to rotate:** frozen roasted veg, riced cauliflower, bagged salad, broccoli slaw, peppers.

## Dinner Menu (~650–700 cal, ~55g protein)

Sheet-pan and one-pan focus — minimal cleanup.

1. **Sheet-pan dinner** — 2 chile lime chicken burgers *or* 3 chicken sausage links + 1½ cups chopped potatoes + 2 cups veg, one tray, 25 min.
2. **Stir-fry** — 6 oz beef or chicken + 2 cups frozen stir-fry veg + ¾ cup rice + 2 tbsp Soyaki or coconut aminos.
3. **Tacos** — 6 oz taco turkey in 3 corn tortillas + 1 cup slaw + 3 tbsp salsa + 2 tbsp guac.
4. **Turkey bolognese** — 6 oz ground turkey + ½ cup marinara over 1 cup cooked pasta (2 oz dry) + side salad.
5. **Dairy-free curry** — 6 oz chicken + ⅓ cup coconut curry sauce + ¾ cup rice + 1 cup frozen veg.
6. **Burger night** — 1 turkey or chile lime burger + bun + 4 oz oven fries + big salad.
7. **Easy button: TJ's frozen Mandarin Orange Chicken** — 1 cup (about ⅓ bag) + ¾ cup rice + 1½ cups steamed frozen veg. Lazy night — pad the veg to stay full.

## Snacks (optional — don't lean on these for calories)

Keep these protein-forward so they help hit 170g rather than just adding calories:

- **MRM egg white protein shake** — 1 scoop in 1 cup water/milk (~24g)
- 2–3 hard-boiled eggs (TJ's sells them pre-cooked)
- Steamed edamame — 1 cup (~17g)
- Turkey/beef jerky or a meat stick — 1 oz jerky / 1 stick
- Hummus (2 tbsp) + carrots/peppers
- 1 apple or banana + 1 tbsp almond/sunflower butter
- Roasted chickpeas — ½ cup
- 1 protein bar (**check for no peanuts/dairy** — many have both)

## Prep Day (Sunday, ~1 hour)

This is what makes the week effortless:

1. **Cook 2 proteins in bulk** — e.g., a tray of chicken + a pan of taco turkey.
2. **Cook 2 carbs** — pot of rice (or just heat frozen) + roast sweet potatoes.
3. **Roast 1 big tray of veg** — whatever's cheap that week.
4. **Hard-boil 6–8 eggs.**
5. Portion into containers OR just keep components separate and assemble bowls each morning in 2 minutes.

Weekday cooking is then either "microwave + assemble" or one fresh pan (eggs, a stir-fry, a sheet-pan).

## Adjusting Over Time

- **Losing faster than ~0.75 lb/week, or lifts getting weaker / sleep worse?** You're cutting too hard — add ~150 cal (a snack or bigger carb portion). Protect the muscle.
- **Scale not moving after 2–3 weeks?** Trim ~150 cal, easiest from carbs at the meal furthest from your workout.
- **Big training day?** Put your largest carb portion around that session for performance.
- **Hungry?** Add volume, not calories: more vegetables, more egg whites, more water. Protein and fibrous veg are the two most filling things you can eat.

**Weigh yourself 3–4 mornings a week and average it.** Daily fluctuations are water — the weekly average is the real trend.
